A Bit About Me...

I became interested in mathematics and computer programming in high school. I got a BS in Mathematics and took intro programming classes at my University in Russia. After that I got a chance to join Intel Corporation as an Intern in Technical Recruiting, and suddenly found myself mastering my skills in hiring top-notch software engineers. Later on, I worked at Sun Microsystems and Oracle Corporation. After moving to the Bay Area, I worked as a technical recruiter with different tech companies across the Silicon Valley.

While I enjoyed working with software developers and hiring managers, I loved technology and problem solving, and decided to switch my career path into software engineering.

As a starting point in my career transition to software engineering I completed Python for Everybody online specialization through Coursera, and earned certificates: Python Data Structures; Using Python to Access Web Data; Using Databases with Python; Data Science Math Skills; Internet History, Technology, and Security.

In August 2018 I completed a three-month software engineering intensive program at Hackbright Academy (curriculum includes Python, JavaScript, SQL, HTML/CSS, and various libraries and frameworks). The last four weeks I spent building a full-stack web app called Moviescope, which utilizes third parties APIs to help users to keep track of films they have watched or want to watch in the future.

I look forward to continuing using my problem-solving mindset along with my new coding skills in a role as a software developer. I am goal-focused, deeply passionate about learning person always ready to contribute to my team's success and go above-and-beyond to achieve corporate objectives.
